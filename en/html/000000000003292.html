i-SHIFT Clutch Actuator/TCM Removal/Installation
NOTE:
  • Take care not to scratch body.
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  1. Make sure you have the anti-theft code for the audio or the navigation system (if equipped), then write down the audio presets. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery first, then disconnect the positive cable. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the harness clip (A), then remove the battery base (B).
  1. Remove the bolts (A) from the slave cylinder (B) so as not to bend the clutch line.
  1. Remove the clutch reservoir tank (A) from the bulkhead (B), then remove the reservoir tube from the clips.
    NOTE:
    • Do not spill brake fluid from the reservoir tank.
    • When remove the reservoir tank, fix the reservoir tank by hand.
  1. Release the lock and remove the clutch actuator connector (A) from its harness clip (B). Disconnect the clutch actuator connector and the ground cable (C).
  1. Release the lock and remove the transmission shift change actuator connector (A), then remove the transmission shift change actuator harness cover (B) and the harness clip (C).
  1. Disconnect the transmission shift change actuator connectors (A).
  1. Raise the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Remove the engine undercover.
  1. Loosen the bolts (A), then remove the clutch actuator (B) with the clutch pipe comp (C) and the clutch fluid tube assembly (D).
  1. Install the clutch actuator in the reverse order of removal.
  1. Bleed the clutch hydraulic system.
  1. Do the i-SHIFT system learning (dynamic mode).
  1. Enter the anti-theft code for the audio or the navigation system, then enter the customer's audio presets, and set the clock.
  1. Perform the power window control unit reset procedure.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle.
