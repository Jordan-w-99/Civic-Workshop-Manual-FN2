DTC Troubleshooting: P19E8 (130-1)
DTC P19E8:
 Vehicle Speed Signal Malfunction (from ECM by Signal Line)
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Raise the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Start the engine, and change the shift lever is in D.
  1. Compair the speedometer indication and the VEHICLE SPEED in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Does the HDS indication match with the speedometer?
YES -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Check the VEHICLE SPEED in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is 0 km/h (0 mph) indicated?
YES -
Repair a short to ground in the wire between the ECM (A32) and the TCM (A79).■
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the clutch actuator subharness 10P connector from the engine compartment wire harness.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between clutch actuator subharness 10P connector terminal No. 5 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair a short to power in the wire between the ECM (A32) and the clutch actuator subharness.■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector A (44P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector A (44P) terminal No. 32 and clutch actuator subharness 10P connector terminal No. 5.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Remove the clutch actuator assembly, and check for clutch actuator subharness. If the wire harness is OK, substitute a known-good TCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good TCM, replace the original TCM. ■
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the ECM (A32) and the clutch actuator subharness.■
