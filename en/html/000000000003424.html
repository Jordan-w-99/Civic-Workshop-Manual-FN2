i-SHIFT System Symptom Troubleshooting - Non-Creep
Non-creep
NOTE: The creep function is only supported in gears, 1, 2, and R.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check these parameters in the DATA LIST with the HDS (check signal change inactive/active):
    • IDLE SWITCH
    • BRAKE SWITCH
    • PARKING BRAKE SWITCH
    • CLUTCH TEMPERATURE (with a clutch temperature of 198 °C (388 °F) creeping torque is reduced linearly. With a clutch temperature of 300 °C (572 °F) creeping function is inactive.)
Do they show the proper valve?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Repair the items that did not show the proper valve.■
  1. Press the brake pedal, and change the shift lever to the A/M position.
  1. Compare the TARGET GEAR and the ACTUAL GEAR in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is the gear position OK?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good TCM, replace the original TCM; R18A2 engine model, L13Z1 engine model. ■
  1. Do the i-SHIFT system learning (static mode).
Is the i-SHIFT system learning OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repeat the i-SHIFT system learning (static mode) and the i-SHIFT system bleeding. ■
  1. Test-drive, under these conditions:
    • Full load start at least five times.
    • Shift to every gear.
    NOTE: During test drive, read out the actual clutch temperature with the HDS. Do not start test drive with clutch temperature of 100 °C (212 °F).
  1. Clear any DTCs with the HDS.
  1. Check the creep condition.
Is it OK?
YES -
Vehicle is normal.■
NO -
Go to Step 1 and recheck.
