Coolant Replacement
  1. Start the engine. Set the heater temperature control dial to maximum heat, then turn off the ignition switch. Make sure the engine and radiator are cool to the touch.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Remove the engine undercover.
  1. Loosen the drain plug, and drain the coolant.
  1. After the coolant has drained, tighten the radiator drain plug.
  1. Remove the coolant reservoir, and drain the coolant, then reinstall the coolant reservoir.
  1. Fill the coolant reservoir to the MAX mark (A) with genuine Honda Long Life Antifreeze/Coolant Type 2 (P/N OL999-9001).
  1. Pour genuine Honda Long Life Antifreeze/Coolant Type 2 (P/N OL999-9001) into the radiator up to the base of the filler neck.
    NOTE:
    • Always use genuine Honda Long Life Antifreeze/Coolant Type 2 (P/N OL999-9001). Using a non-Honda coolant can result in corrosion, causing the cooling system to malfunction or fail.
    • Genuine Honda Long Life Antifreeze/Coolant Type 2 is a mixture of 50 % antifreeze and 50 % water. Do not add water.
    Engine Coolant Capacities (Including the coolant reservoir capacity of 0.5 L (0.13 US gal, 0.11 Imp gal))
    At Coolant Change: 4.4 L (1.16 US gal, 0.97 Imp gal)
    After Engine Overhaul: 6.6 L (1.74 US gal, 1.45 Imp gal)

  1. Loosely install the radiator cap.
  1. Start the engine, and let it run until it warms up (the radiator fan comes on at least twice).
  1. Turn off the engine. Check the level in the radiator and add Honda Long Life Antifreeze/Coolant Type 2 if needed.
  1. Put the radiator cap on tightly, then run the engine again, and check for leaks.
  1. Install the engine undercover.
