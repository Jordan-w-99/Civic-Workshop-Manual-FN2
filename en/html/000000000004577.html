VSA DTC Troubleshooting: 12, 14, 16, 18
DTC 12, 14, 16, 18:
 Wheel Sensor (Electrical Noise/Intermittent Interruption)
NOTE: If the ABS and other indicators come on because of electrical noise, the indicators will go off when you test-drive the vehicle at 15 km/h (10 mph) or more and noise is gone.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Check for DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 11, 13, 15, and/or 17 indicated with DTC 12, 14, 16, and/or 18 at a time?
YES -
Do the DTC 11, 13, 15, and/or 17 troubleshooting. ■
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the appropriate wheel sensor and magnetic encoder on the wheel bearing or pulser on the hub bearing unit.

  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle at 15 km/h (10 mph) or more.
  1. Check for DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 12, 14, 16, and/or 18 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for loose terminals between the wheel sensor 2P connector and the VSA modulator-control unit 46P connector. Refer to intermittent failures troubleshooting. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Substitute a known-good wheel sensor for the appropriate wheel sensor.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle at 15 km/h (10 mph) or more.
  1. Check for DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 12, 14, 16, and/or 18 indicated?
YES -
Check for loose terminals in the VSA modulator-control unit 46P connector. If necessary, substitute a known-good VSA modulator-control unit and retest.■
